Climate

Bhadradri Kothagudem experiences a hot tropical climate, with very hot summers, humid monsoons, and mild winters. The region receives heavy rainfall due to its forest cover and proximity to the Godavari basin.

How to Reach Badradri Kothagudem

Bhadradri-Kothagudem is one of India’s best-connected cities by air, rail and road.

By Air

Rajiv Gandhi International Airport
Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (Shamshabad) is about 30 km from the city centre and is one of India’s busiest international airports with excellent domestic and international connectivity.

By Rail

The nearest major railway station is Bhadrachalam Road (Kothagudem) Railway Station (Station Code: BDCR), offering connectivity to Hyderabad, Vijayawada, Kazipet, and other key routes.
Main station: Hyderabad Deccan (Nampally). Other major terminals include Secunderabad Jn and Kacheguda, connecting Hyderabad to all major Indian cities.

By Road

Bhadradri Kothagudem is well-connected by TSRTC buses and national/state highways, linking it to Hyderabad, Khammam, Warangal, and nearby districts.
Bhadradri Kothagudem is well-connected by TSRTC buses and highways. It has rail access via Bhadrachalam Road Station, while the nearest airport is Hyderabad. Local travel is easy with autos and taxis.

Top Attractions in Badradri Kothagudem

Heritage monuments, lakes, museums, temples, zoos and cultural hubs —BADRADRI KOTHAGUDEM has it all.

Sri Sita Ramachandra Swamy Temple, Bhadrachalam

Bhadrachalam, on the banks of the Godavari River, is Telangana’s most sacred Vaishnavite shrine dedicated to Lord Rama and Goddess Sita. The temple, built in the 17th century by Bhakta Ramadasu, showcases exquisite Dravidian architecture and spiritual grandeur. The annual Sri Rama Navami Kalyanam attracts thousands of pilgrims. The serene Godavari ghats nearby offer boat rides and holy dips. Blending devotion, heritage, Bhadrachalam remains the spiritual heart of Bhadradri Kothagudem.

Parnasala

Parnasala, about 35 km from Bhadrachalam, is a legendary site from the Ramayana where Lord Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana are believed to have lived during exile. It is also said that Ravana abducted Sita here. The site features beautifully carved huts, murals, and sculptures depicting episodes from the epic. Set amidst forests and the Godavari River, Parnasala offers a divine, serene landscape ideal for pilgrims and history enthusiasts. The mix of mythology and natural beauty makes it a top cultural and spiritual destination in Bhadradri Kothagudem District.

Kinnerasani wildlife sanctuary

Situated near Palvancha, Kinnerasani wildlife sanctuary the spans over 600 sq km along the Godavari River basin. It shelters species such as deer, wild boars, leopards, peacocks, and many birds amid dense teak and bamboo forests. The Kinnerasani Dam and Lake inside the sanctuary enhance its scenic charm. Visitors can enjoy boating, forest treks, and viewpoints overlooking the reservoir. Eco-tourism cottages and watchtowers are available for nature lovers. The surroundings make it a perfect getaway for wildlife enthusiasts in the district.

Dummugudem Lift Irrigation Project

Located on the Godavari River, Dummugudem Project is a major irrigation structure and scenic location in Bhadradri Kothagudem. The wide reservoir surrounded by greenery offers picturesque landscapes and peaceful drives along the riverbanks. It plays a vital role in regional agriculture while also serving as a relaxing picnic spot. The site is ideal for sunset views, photography, and understanding Telangana’s water-resource development. The calm river flow and rural backdrop provide a balance of engineering wonder and natural beauty.

Gundala Hot Springs

Near Aswaraopeta Mandal, Gundala is famous for its natural hot springs located amid thick forests along the Godavari tributaries. The springs are believed to have medicinal properties that help cure skin and joint ailments. Visitors can enjoy warm baths while surrounded by serene forested hills. The site also attracts researchers studying geothermal activity. Simple yet refreshing, Gundala Hot Springs offer a rare combination of wellness, nature, and tranquillity. It remains one of Bhadradri Kothagudem’s hidden natural treasures.

Kothagudem Thermal Power Station (Palvancha)

Established in 1966, the Kothagudem Thermal Power Station is one of Telangana’s oldest and most important power-generation units. Situated near Palvancha, it symbolizes industrial progress in the state. While entry is restricted, the massive chimneys, power lines, and organized township make it a notable landmark. The surrounding landscape, including parks and reservoirs, provides a unique mix of industry and greenery. For visitors interested in engineering or energy infrastructure, this site represents the backbone of Telangana’s electricity production.

Aswaraopeta Tribal Village and Forests

Aswaraopeta, located on the state border, offers a glimpse into the tribal culture and forest ecosystems of Bhadradri Kothagudem. Surrounded by scenic hills, coffee plantations, and thick forest, it is home to Koya and other tribal communities known for their traditional art and festivals. Visitors can explore local markets, bamboo crafts, and forest walks. The area’s cool climate and natural charm make it suitable for eco-tourism and cultural exploration. Aswaraopeta represents the district’s harmony between people and nature.

Rock Art Sites (Damaratogu)

The Damaratogu Rock Art Sites are a significant archaeological find in the Gundala Mandal of the Bhadradri Kothagudem District, Telangana. Archaeologists believe the artwork dates back to the Mesolithic Age (8000–3000 BCE), given the style and subject matter. The paintings are unique because they exclusively depict animals and deliberately omit human figures, weapons, or domestic animals. This thematic focus suggests a cultural practice possibly related to early hunting rituals or animal worship, providing vital clues about the hunter-gatherer societies that inhabited the Godavari valley thousands of years ago.

Culture & Festivals

Badradri Kothagudem eflects tribal and Telugu culture. Festivals like Sri Rama Navami, Bonalu, Dussehra, and tribal celebrations are observed with great enthusiasm.

  • Languages: Telugu, Urdu, lambadi,koya and English are widely spoken.
  • Heritage:Bhadradri Kothagudem is known for the famous Bhadrachalam Rama Temple and the rich traditions of Koya and Lambadi tribes. Forests, temples, and tribal culture shape its heritage.
  • Major Festivals: Sri rama Navami,, Bonalu, Diwali, Bathukamma,and many local cultural events.

Getting Around Within Badradri Kothagudem

Badradri Kothagudem has multiple options for moving around the city comfortably and affordably.

  • City Buses: TSRTC operates extensive city buses including Pushpak airport services.
  • Cabs & Bike Taxis: Ola, Uber and Rapido work across most parts of the city.
  • Auto-rickshaws: Widely available and convenient for short-distance travel.
