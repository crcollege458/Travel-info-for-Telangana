SIDDIPET

Siddipet is a newly formed district in the Indian state of Telangana, established on October 11, 2016, with its headquarters located in Siddipet town. It is known for its rich cultural heritage, ancient religious attractions, and significant urban development.

How to Reach Siddipet

Siddipet is one of Telangana's best-connected cities by air, rail and road.

By Air

Rajiv Gandhi International Airport
To reach Siddipet by air, fly into the nearest airport, Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD) in Hyderabad, and then take a taxi or bus for the remaining 147 km to Siddipet

By Rail

Secunderabad
You can reach Siddipet by taking a train from Secunderabad to Siddipet Railway Station (SIPT), as it is the closest railway station. From Secunderabad, you can board one of the daily DEMU trains to Siddipet, which takes approximately 3 hours. For other train routes or further connectivity, you will need to travel by road from the closest railway station.

By Road

NH-365B
To reach Siddipet by road, you can drive via highways from nearby cities like Hyderabad, Warangal, or Karimnagar, using state highways and national highway corridors. From Hyderabad, a common route involves the Outer Ring Road and the state highway towards Siddipet.

Komati Cheruvu

A major attraction within Siddipet city, the lake is complemented by a 241-metre long suspension bridge on a 4-foot wide pedestrian track. It offers scenic evening walks, boating, and the light-illuminated bridge makes for a pleasant outing for families. The lake area is also linked with other leisure spaces, making it a lively local hangout and an easy stop for visitors.

Next to Komati Cheruvu, this illuminated park features life-sized animal statues, laser water-fountain shows, and twilight lights that create a ‚Äúnight-garden‚Äù experience. With its 3-D screens and attractions targeted for children and families, it offers a modern recreational site amid the heritage and nature spots of the district. Ranganayaka Sagar Reservoir

Constructed under the Kaleshwaram Lift Irrigation Scheme, this large waterbody in Chinnakodur Mandal offers scenic drives, water views, and an hour-long stretch of road along one side of the reservoir. Ideal for an early morning or sunset outing, it provides a calm escape with natural beauty and open-space feeling away from city bustle.

Oxygen Park

This well-maintained botanical green space spans approx. 205 hectares and features children‚Äôs play areas, life-sized animal installations, a watchtower, and open-air exercise zones. A family-friendly destination to relax, take walks, enjoy nature, and unwind in greenery within the district.

Sri Vidya Saraswati Temple

On a hillock near Wargal village, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Saraswati and is one of the few such shrines in Telangana. Devotees bring children here for the Akshara-abhyasa (commencement of learning) ritual. The complex also includes shrines to Ganapati, Shani, and Shiva. A tranquil and spiritually meaningful site with scenic views.

Sri Mallikarjuna Swamy Devasthanam

Situated in Komuravelli village of Siddipet district, this hill-temple is devoted to Lord Mallanna (Mallikarjuna). Known for folk traditions like Oggu Katha singing, the annual jathara (festival) and the spiritual vibe draw many devotees.The hill setting provides a mix of devotion and nature for visitors.

Kondapochamma Temple

Located in Markook Mandal of Siddipet district, this temple is dedicated to the local goddess Kondapochamma, popular among tribal and rural communities. The temple setting amidst villages offers a genuine rural pilgrimage experience and insight into local devotional practices and village culture.

Nagadevatha Temple

One of the traditional village temples listed among points of interest in the district, this temple dedicated to Nagadevatha (serpent-goddess) reflects rural faith practices in Siddipet district. While not as large as major pilgrimage sites, the temple provides context into folk-religion and the cultural fabric of the region.

Culture & Festivals

Siddipet's culture is rich in festivals, particularly the flower-based Bathukamma celebration, and is deeply connected to the Gollabhama saree tradition and the worship of local deities like Mallanna at the Komuravelli temple.

  • Languages: Telugu, Urdu and Lambadi are widely spoken.
  • Heritage & Arts: Archaeological Sites,Historical Monuments, mosques and religious temples.
  • Major Festivals: Bathukamma floral festival, the Bonalu festival, and the Komuravelli Mallanna Jathara and many local cultural eventsare celebrated.

Getting Around Within Siddipet

Siddipet has multiple options for moving around the city comfortably and affordably.

  • City Buses:TGSRTC operates various bus types, including ordinary, express, deluxe, and AC/non-AC sleeper buses.
  • Cabs & Bike Taxis: Ola, Uber and Rapido work across most parts of the city.
  • Auto-rickshaws: Widely available and convenient for short-distance travel.
