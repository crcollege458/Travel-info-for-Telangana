HYDERABAD — Heart of Telangana

Hyderabad, the capital city of Telangana, is a historic and modern metropolis known for its iconic monuments, IT hubs, rich culture, and world-famous cuisine. Famous for its Nizami legacy, it beautifully blends heritage structures with advanced infrastructure, making it one of India’s most dynamic urban destinations.

How to Reach Hyderabad

Hyderabad is one of India’s best-connected cities by air, rail and road.

By Air

Rajiv Gandhi International Airport
Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (Shamshabad) is about 30 km from the city centre and is one of India’s busiest international airports with excellent domestic and international connectivity.

By Rail

Hyd, Secunderabad, Kacheguda
Main station: Hyderabad Deccan (Nampally). Other major terminals include Secunderabad Jn and Kacheguda, connecting Hyderabad to all major Indian cities.

By Road

NH-44 • NH-65 • ORR
Hyderabad is well connected by road via National Highways NH-44 and NH-65, the Outer Ring Road (ORR), and PVNR Expressway, with frequent TSRTC and private buses.

Top Attractions in Hyderabad

Heritage monuments, lakes, museums, temples, zoos and cultural hubs — Hyderabad has it all.

Charminar

Built in 1591 by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, Charminar is Hyderabad’s most iconic monument with four grand minarets and splendid Indo-Islamic–Persian architecture, surrounded by the bustling Laad Bazaar.

Golconda Fort

A massive fortress renowned for its acoustic wonders, royal palaces and evening light & sound show. It is also part of India’s tentative UNESCO list along with Charminar and Qutb Shahi Tombs.

Qutb Shahi Tombs

A stunning complex of tombs and mosques of the Qutb Shahi dynasty in Ibrahim Bagh, showcasing Indo-Islamic architecture with graceful domes, galleries and once richly tiled exteriors.

Chowmahalla Palace

Chowmahalla Palace in Hyderabad, once the se="at of the Nizams, is renowned for its elegant courtyards, grand Durbar Hall, and Indo-Persian architecture. Built in the 18th century, it showcases royal artifacts, intricate design, and cultural heritage, offering visitors a glimpse into the opulent lifestyle of Hyderabad’s erstwhile rulers and legacy.

Hussain Sagar Lake & Tank Bund

A 16th-century lake linking Hyderabad and Secunderabad, famous for the giant Buddha statue, boating, laser shows, Lumbini Park and Necklace Road — especially beautiful at sunset.

Salar Jung Museum

One of India’s largest art museums, home to global artifacts, sculptures, paintings, clocks and the famed “Veiled Rebecca”, located close to Charminar and Mecca Masjid.

Birla Mandir

A serene white-marble hilltop temple dedicated to Lord Venkateswara on Naubath Pahad, blending Dravidian, Rajasthani and Utkala styles, offering panoramic views of the city and Hussain Sagar.

Nehru Zoological Park

Spread over 380 acres near Mir Alam Tank, this zoo features tiger & lion safaris, butterfly park, aquarium, toy train, boating and a wildlife museum — perfect for families and students.

Shilparamam

An arts and crafts village in Madhapur showcasing traditional handicrafts, handlooms, folk art, rural-style huts, sculptures and cultural performances across 65 acres.

Lumbini Park

A lakeside urban park known for musical and 3D laser shows, boating to the Buddha statue, manicured gardens and a floral clock — ideal for evening outings with family.

Culture & Festivals

Hyderabad carries a strong Nizami influence in its architecture, language, food and everyday traditions. The city is a blend of old-world charm and cosmopolitan lifestyle where minarets, palaces, IT parks and shopping malls coexist.

  • Languages: Telugu, Urdu, Hindi and English are widely spoken.
  • Heritage: Nizami architecture, palaces, mosques and old bazaars around the Charminar area.
  • Major Festivals: Ramzan, Bonalu, Diwali, Bathukamma, Eid-ul-Adha and many local cultural events.

Getting Around Within Hyderabad

Hyderabad has multiple options for moving around the city comfortably and affordably.

  • Metro: Hyderabad Metro covers key corridors like Miyapur–LB Nagar, JBS–MGBS, and Nagole–Hi-Tech City.
  • City Buses: TSRTC operates extensive city buses including Pushpak airport services.
  • Cabs & Bike Taxis: Ola, Uber and Rapido work across most parts of the city.
  • Auto-rickshaws: Widely available and convenient for short-distance travel.

Best Time to Visit Hyderabad

October to February

These months offer pleasant weather, ideal for sightseeing, outdoor activities and heritage walks.
