Siricilla ‚Äî Texile Town of Telangana

Sircilla, located in Telangana, is famous for its vibrant textile industry and is popularly called the **Textile Town** of the state. It is a major hub for power looms, dyeing units, and modern textile production. The town has grown rapidly with strong support from local artisans and government initiatives. Its weaving community has a long cultural history, blending traditional skills with new technology. Today, Sircilla stands as a symbol of craftsmanship, innovation, and economic progress in Telangana. üßµ

Top Attractions in Siricilla

Heritage monuments, lakes, museums, temples, zoos and cultural hubs ‚Äî Siricilla has it all.

Sri Raja Rajeswara Swamy Temple (Vemulawada)

This ancient town is home to the Sri Raja Rajeswara Swamy Temple, often called ‚ÄúDakshina Kasi‚Äù.when Vemulawada was the capital of the rulers. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva (Rajarajeshwara) and also houses shrines of Raja¬≠Rajeswari Devi and Ganesha. Devotees flock here during festivals like Maha Shivaratri, and the holy tank ‚ÄúDharma Gundam‚Äù adds spiritual ambience.

Ananthagiri Reservoir

Located near Sircilla town, this reservoir offers scenic water vistas and a calm break from city bustle. The area around the lake is good for early morning walks, photography of water reflections, and enjoying a sundowner beside the still water. While not as developed as major tourist lakes, its proximity to Sircilla town makes it a convenient destination for a short outing.

Chennur Fort

Chennur Mandal hosts this historical fort, one of the lesser-visited heritage monuments in the district. The old fort-walls and nearby temple ruins give insight into pre-modern rule and local power-centres. Visitors interested in history, ruins, photography and quiet walks will find this site worthwhile. Combine it with nearby village visits for fuller experience.

Nampally Gutta & Lakshmi Narasimha swamy temple

Nampally Gutta, near Vemulawada in Telangana, is a peaceful hillock known for the Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple. Visitors climb a short pathway and pass through a unique snake-shaped cave. Its scenic views and spiritual ambiance make it a popular pilgrimage spot.

Vemulawada tank

The Vemulawada Tank, also called Gudi Cheruvu or Dharmagundam, is a sacred water body adjacent to the Sri Raja Rajeshwara Swamy Temple in Vemulawada, Telangana. Devotees traditionally take a holy dip here before temple worship, believing its waters possess spiritual and medicinal significance. It remains central to religious, cultural, and local irrigation activities.

samudralingam waterfalls

The Samudralingam Waterfall, a beautiful seasonal cascade, is a prominent natural attraction located in the Rajanna Sircilla District of Telangana, near the village of Samudralingapur. This serene spot is highly recommended for nature enthusiasts, particularly between July and October when the monsoon rains ensure a robust flow, transforming it into a vibrant, picturesque destination perfect for photography and tranquil retreats.

Annapurna reserviour

The Annapurna Reservoir is a critical infrastructural asset located predominantly in the Rajanna Sircilla District of Telangana, near the village of Thippapur. As a vital component of the massive Kaleshwaram Lift Irrigation Project (KLIP), it plays a crucial role in water storage and distribution. The reservoir's future impact is significant: it ensures irrigation stability for farmers in multiple districts and stands as a testament to the state's commitment to water security and sustainable agricultural prosperity, mitigating drought risks for generations to come.

Sri Baddi Pochamma Temple

Sri Baddi Pochamma Temple, a village deity shrine in Vemulawada, was built by Rashtrakuta King Baddiga Bhupathi. It is dedicated to Pochamma Devi, enshrined as a granite idol of Sitala Devi with weapons. The temple is a significant site for pilgrims who visit to offer Bonalu for health and happiness. Located about 1.5 km from the bus stand, it is easily accessible for visitors exploring the area's rich heritage.

Sri Bheemeshwara Swamy Temple

The Sri Bheemeshwara Swamy temple is a significant ancient shrine in Vemulawada, Telangana, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Built in the 9th century CE by the Chalukya king Bhaddega, it showcases the beautiful Nagara architectural style. The temple, a sub-shrine of the main Sri Raja Rajeshwara Swamy temple complex, features a large black stone Shiva Lingam in its sanctum.

Culture & Festivals

Sircilla reflects the rich weaving heritage of Telangana, where the rhythm of power looms and the legacy of skilled artisans shape everyday life. The town blends traditional craftsmanship with modern industrial growth, earning its name as the Textile Town of Telangana. Here, handlooms, temples, vibrant local markets, and developing urban spaces coexist, giving Sircilla a character that is both culturally rooted and forward-moving.

  • Languages: Telugu, Urdu, Hindi and English are widely spoken.
  • Heritage:Textile traditions, power-loom clusters, vibrant markets and the steady hum of weaving units in Sircilla are living symbols of its rich artisan heritage, preserving generations of craftsmanship even as the town grows into a modern textile hub.
  • Major Festivals: sankranthi,ugadi,dusshera,bathukamma,bonalu and local texile community celebrations

Getting Around Within Siricilla

Sircilla offers simple and convenient options for moving around the town comfortably and affordably.

  • City Buses: TSRTC operates regular buses connecting major areas of Sircilla with surrounding towns like Vemulawada, Karimnagar and Siddipet.
  • Local Autos: Auto-rickshaws are widely available and are the most common and convenient option for short-distance travel.
  • Cabs & Bike Taxis: Services like Rapido operate in Sircilla, offering quick and affordable point-to-point rides.
  • Shared Transport: Shared autos and local jeeps are frequently used for nearby villages and daily commuting.
  • Intercity Connectivity: Sircilla is well connected by road to major cities through TSRTC and private travel services.
