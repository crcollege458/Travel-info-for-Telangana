YADHADRI BHUVANAGIRI — Worship of Telangana

"Yadadri Bhuvanagiri, located in Telangana about 60–80 km northeast of Hyderabad, is a district known for its spiritual heritage, historic forts, and scenic landscapes. The district is home to the revered Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple at Yadagirigutta, attracting thousands of pilgrims each year, and the ancient Bhongir Fort, built on a monolithic rock, which offers stunning views and a glimpse into the region's rich history. The district retains strong Telugu and rural Telangana traditions, with festivals such as Bathukamma, Bonalu, and Brahmotsavam celebrated with great fervor. Agriculture forms the backbone of the local economy, with crops like paddy, maize, and pulses widely cultivated, while religious tourism and small-scale industries contribute to growth. Visitors can also explore the natural beauty of the hills, villages, and forests surrounding the district, making Yadadri Bhuvanagiri a blend of culture, history, spirituality, and nature."

How to Reach Yadadri Bhuvanagiri

Yadadri Bhuvanagiri is well-connected by road and rail, with easy access from Hyderabad and surrounding regions.

By Air

Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (Hyderabad)
The nearest airport is Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (Shamshabad), about 90–100 km from Yadadri. From the airport, taxis, cabs, and TSRTC buses connect to Yadadri via Hyderabad city.

By Rail

Bhongir • Raigir • Aler Stations
Bhongir Railway Station is the major station connecting Yadadri with Hyderabad, Warangal, Vijayawada, and other regional cities. Raigir Railway Station offers direct access to the Yadadri temple area.

By Road

NH-163 • ORR • Yadadri Main Road
Yadadri is easily accessible by road from Hyderabad via NH-163. TSRTC runs frequent buses from Secunderabad, Uppal, LB Nagar, and other parts of Hyderabad. The drive via ORR–Bhongir route offers smooth connectivity.

Top Attractions in Yadhadri Bhuvanagiri

Heritage monuments, lakes, museums, temples, zoos and cultural hubs — Yadhadri Bhuvanagiri has it all.

Yadagirigutta (Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple)

Yadagirigutta, now officially known as Yadadri, is the spiritual heart of Telangana. The grand hilltop temple dedicated to Lord Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy is built on a cave, where the deity is believed to have manifested in five forms. The recently renovated temple showcases magnificent stone architecture, gold-plated domes, and spacious mandapams. Pilgrims climb the steps or use rope cars to reach the shrine. The area around the temple has been transformed into a spiritual township with wide roads, parks, and guest houses. The temple attracts thousands daily and stands as a symbol of devotion and cultural identity for Telangana

-Bhongir Fort (Bhuvanagiri Fort)

Bhongir Fort, built in the 10th century by the Western Chalukyas, crowns a massive monolithic rock rising 500 feet above the plains. The fort’s oval structure, double ramparts, hidden passages, and panoramic views make it an architectural wonder. The climb to the top involves stone steps winding around boulders and offers sweeping vistas of Bhongir town. Once a military stronghold, the fort also features watchtowers and reservoirs. Today it’s a favorite spot for trekking, history enthusiasts, and photographers. At sunrise or sunset, the golden hues on the granite rock make Bhongir Fort a mesmerizing sight.

Kolanupaka Jain Temple

Kolanupaka Jain Temple, about 20 km from Bhongir, is one of South India’s most sacred Jain pilgrimage sites. Believed to be over 2,000 years old, it houses an 18-foot tall idol of Lord Rishabhanatha carved from a single piece of jade. The temple’s walls display exquisite carvings depicting Jain Tirthankaras, floral designs, and ancient inscriptions. The tranquil surroundings, polished marble floors, and ornate gopurams create a serene atmosphere. Adjacent temples for Lord Adinatha, Lord Neminatha, and Lord Mahavira complete the complex. Kolanupaka represents Telangana’s religious diversity and artistic brilliance in stone.

Surendrapuri (Kunda Satyanarayana Kaladhamam)

Located near Yadagirigutta, Surendrapuri is a mythological theme park created by Kunda Satyanarayana. The vast complex recreates scenes from Indian epics – Ramayana, Mahabharata, and Bhagavata Purana – through 3D sculptures, paintings, and temple replicas. The main temple houses Panchamukha Hanuman and Navagraha shrines. A museum showcases vivid mythological displays and cosmic models. Visitors experience India’s spiritual heritage through art, education, and devotion. Its clean surroundings and detailed craftsmanship make it a must-visit for families and pilgrims heading to Yadadri.

Baswapur Reservoir (Mini Tank Bund)

The Baswapur Reservoir, often referred to locally as the Baswapur Tank or Mini Tank Bund, is a significant water storage facility in the Yadadri Bhuvanagiri District of Telangana. Located near Baswapur village, it serves as a critical off-take reservoir within the massive Kaleshwaram Lift Irrigation Scheme (KLIS), ensuring stable water supply for the region's agriculture. Due to its vast, scenic expanse, the government is actively developing the Baswapur Reservoir into a major recreational and tourist hub, offering potential amenities like boating and parks. It stands as a vital component of the district's new water and tourism infrastruture

Yadadri View Point and Hill Road

The scenic drive to Yadadri temple through the winding Hill Road is a visitor attraction in itself. The route passes through rocky hills, gardens, and viewpoints where travelers stop for photos of the temple complex and surrounding plains. Sunrise and sunset along this stretch offer panoramic golden views. The road has been newly developed with railings, lighting, and rest stops, making it one of the most picturesque temple approaches in Telangana. It’s perfect for road-trippers and pilgrims alike seeking both devotion and natural beauty.

Raigiri Fort and Temples

Raigiri Fort, located 5 km from Bhongir, is another medieval fortification built by the Kakatiyas. It features rock-cut steps, ancient watchtowers, and shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva and Hanuman. The fort’s elevated position gives a 360-degree view of the surrounding plains and nearby villages. Though partly in ruins, it offers excellent trekking and photography opportunities. The fort area is surrounded by small ponds, adding to the scenic charm. Raigiri Fort provides a glimpse into Telangana’s feudal history and architectural strength.

Rachakonda Fort

Rachakonda Fort, situated in the southern part of Yadadri Bhuvanagiri District, was built by Recherla Singaraya Nayaka in the 14th century. Constructed entirely of massive stones without mortar, it exemplifies early Telangana’s military architecture. The fort’s twin hills are connected by a stone pathway leading to gateways with Telugu inscriptions. Visitors can enjoy trekking, explore ancient fort walls, and view the surrounding Aravalli-like hills. The peaceful atmosphere, scenic backdrop, and historical charm make Rachakonda a must-visit heritage and adventure spot in the district.

Swarnagiri Venkateswara Swamy Temple

The Swarnagiri Venkateswara Swamy Temple, also known as Yadadri Tirumala Devasthanam, is a magnificent and recently constructed Hindu shrine in Bhuvanagiri, Telangana, dedicated to Lord Venkateswara. Situated atop the Manepally Hills, this architectural marvel spans 22 acres and showcases a blend of Pallava, Vijayanagara, Chola, and Chalukya styles. Key features include four grand Rajagopurams and a five-story Vimana Gopuram. The temple houses a majestic 12-foot-tall black granite statue of Lord Venkateswara, the largest in Telangana, and a notable 40-foot monolithic statue of Hanuman. It has quickly emerged as a major spiritual and cultural center.

Bhuvanagiri Sri Guru Raghavendra Swamy Temple

Bhuvanagiri Sri Ragavendra Swamy's Birth Place...Maha ...The Sri Guru Raghavendra Swamy Temple in Bhuvanagiri is the birthplace of the 17th-century saint, philosopher, and scholar, Sri Raghavendra Swamy. The temple complex includes the house where he was born and a brindavan (a sacred spot where a holy person attained samadhi). It is a significant pilgrimage site for devotees who believe in his miraculous deeds and teachings on devotion to Vishnu.

Culture & Festivals

Yadadri Bhuvanagiri is known for its deep-rooted Telugu traditions, temple culture, and vibrant rural heritage. The district is spiritually significant, with the revered Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple at Yadagirigutta serving as its cultural and religious centre.

  • Languages: Telugu is predominantly spoken, along with Urdu and Hindi in some areas.
  • Heritage: Temple architecture, historic forts like Bhongir Fort, traditional crafts, and cultural practices reflecting Telangana’s heritage.
  • Major Festivals: Brahmotsavam at Yadadri, Bonalu, Bathukamma, Vaikunta Ekadasi, Deepavali, Sri Rama Navami, and other local temple festivals.
