ADILABAD — Gateway of Telangan

Adilabad is a district in Telangana, a region bordering Maharashtra, known as the "gateway to South India". It is currently home to the Kawal Tiger Reserve and is a part of the "red corridor". The district's economy is supported by agriculture, and the primary languages spoken are Telugu and Urdu, followed by Marathi, Hindi, and Gondi.

How to Reach Adilabad

Adilabad is growing in connectivity by rail and road; air connectivity is under development.

By Air

Proposed Adilabad Airport
A new airport in Adilabad has been approved with land acquisition of approx.700 acres underway. Until it is operational, the nearest major airport remains Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (Hyderabad) about 310 km away.

By Rail

Adilabad Station
Adilabad has a railway station on the Mudkhed–Majri section of the South Central Railway, connecting to major cities and regions.

By Road

NH-44 • NH-353B
Adilabad is well connected by national highways including NH 44 and NH 353B, facilitating bus and road-travel across Telangana and neighbouring states.

Top Attractions in Adilabad

Heritage monuments, lakes, museums, temples, zoos and cultural hubs Adilabad has it all.

Kuntala Waterfalls

Kuntala Waterfalls, located near Neradigonda in Adilabad district, is Telangana’s highest waterfall, cascading from a height of about 45 meters. Formed by the Kadam River, it is surrounded by thick forests and rocky cliffs. During monsoon, the water gushes with tremendous force, creating a breathtaking view. According to legend, Shakuntala and King Dushyanta once met here, giving the waterfall its name. Visitors can trek down stone steps to reach the base for scenic views. With its misty spray, cool breeze, and lush green valley, Kuntala is Adilabad’s most famous natural attraction.

Pochera Waterfalls

Pochera Waterfalls, about 40 km from Adilabad town, is a multi-tiered natural cascade of the Godavari River. Falling from a height of 20 meters over rocky granite terrain, it forms deep blue pools below. The thick forest surrounding it adds mystery and charm, making it ideal for picnics and photography. During monsoon, the falls roar magnificently, offering a thrilling sight. Well-paved paths lead visitors close to the water. The serene forest soundscape and shimmering mist make Pochera one of Adilabad’s most captivating destinations for nature lovers and weekend travelers.

Kawal wildlife sanctuary

Kawal Wildlife Sanctuary, now a Tiger Reserve, is one of Telangana’s largest forest ecosystems, spreading across Adilabad and neighboring districts. The sanctuary shelters tigers, leopards, sloth bears, deer, and over 250 species of birds. Dense teak, bamboo, and deciduous trees form its green canopy. Eco-tourism activities such as jungle safaris, bird-watching, and nature camps are organized by the forest department. It plays a crucial role in wildlife conservation and environmental education. The tranquil forest and the thrill of spotting animals make it a must-visit for nature enthusiasts.

Jainath temple

Jainath Temple, 21 km from Adilabad town, is an ancient Shiva temple dating back to the Satavahana and Chalukyan periods. It is famous for its Prakrit inscriptions and intricate stone carvings depicting mythological scenes. The temple’s unique feature is its open-air sanctum, where sunlight directly falls on the deity during Karthika Pournami, drawing large crowds for the annual fair. Surrounded by peaceful countryside, the temple reflects Telangana’s early art and architectural excellence. Jainath remains a sacred and historical highlight of Adilabad district.

Mavala Lake and Park

Located just 3 km from Adilabad town, Mavala Lake is a beautiful man-made reservoir surrounded by small hills and plantations. It’s a popular evening spot for locals and tourists alike. Telangana Tourism has developed the site with a walking track, boating, and landscaped gardens. The lake’s still waters mirror the golden hues of sunset, creating a perfect scene for photographers. Families enjoy picnics along the shore, and the nearby park offers safe play areas for children. Mavala Lake provides a calm, refreshing escape from city bustle.

Gayatri Waterfalls

Hidden within the deep forest near Tarnam Khurd village, Gayatri Waterfall is one of Adilabad’s most enchanting natural wonders. The Kadem River plunges from 30 meters into a rocky gorge surrounded by thick vegetation. The trek to the waterfall passes through scenic forest trails, bamboo groves, and streams. The crystal-clear pool at the base invites visitors for a refreshing dip. The sound of cascading water and chirping birds makes it an unforgettable nature experience. It’s a true hidden gem for adventure and photography enthusiasts.

Kanakai Waterfalls (Kanakadurga Falls)

Located near Girnoor village, Kanakai Waterfall is a beautiful multi-tiered fall surrounded by rocky cliffs and forest. Named after the nearby Kanaka Durga temple, it’s a place of both natural and spiritual significance. A short trek through greenery leads to the cascading falls, especially vibrant during monsoon. Visitors can enjoy scenic photography, picnics, and light hiking. The cool breeze and tranquil environment make it ideal for relaxation. Kanakai Waterfall showcases the untouched natural beauty of northern Telangana.

Sathnala Dam and Eco Park

Sathnala Dam, about 35 km from Adilabad town, is a charming picnic spot nestled between forested hills. The reservoir, built on the Sathnala River, irrigates nearby fields and supports aquatic life. Telangana Tourism has developed an eco-park here with gardens, walkways, and viewpoints overlooking the shimmering lake. Migratory birds visit during winter, adding to the scenic charm. The peaceful atmosphere, boating options, and lush surroundings make Sathnala Dam a perfect one-day outing for families and nature enthusiasts.

Nagoba temple

The Nagoba temple in Keslapur, Adilabad district, is a significant pilgrimage centre for the Mesram clan of Gonds and is renowned for the annual Nagoba Jatara festival. It is a unique cultural destination where visitors can witness the rich traditions and rituals of the Adivasi communities. The serene, rustic setting offers a peaceful and spiritually enriching experience, a stark contrast to urban life. In the future, the temple and festival's growing popularity can significantly boost cultural tourism, providing economic benefits and preserving tribal heritage. Sustainable development and sensitive management are crucial to handle the increasing number of visitors without negatively impacting the community's way of life or the environment, ensuring the unique cultural integrity of the site is maintained.

Girnnor waterfalls

The Girnnor Waterfalls, locally known as Kanakai Waterfalls, are a stunning natural spectacle in the Adilabad district, especially vibrant during and after the monsoon season. Nestled amid dense forests, they offer a picturesque and tranquil escape for nature lovers and trekkers. The site also features a Kanakadurga temple, adding a cultural dimension to the natural beauty. It is an ideal spot for photography, picnicking, and enjoying the raw beauty of Telangana's landscapes.

Culture & Festivals

Adilabad carries a rich tribal and rural heritage, where the traditions of the Gond people and other Adivasi communities are woven into everyday life. The district is known for its folk dances, tribal festivals, crafts and shared cultural celebrations.

  • Languages: Telugu is predominant, with significant influence of Gondi and other tribal dialects, plus Urdu and Hindi spoken in many parts.

Shopping & Local Bazaars – Adilabad

Explore the most famous shopping spots in Adilabad, known for clothing, accessories and local markets.

Anand Shopping Mall

A popular local choice with a 3.7 rating, Anand Shopping Mall is well-known for affordable clothing, daily-use items, and a variety of small local stores catering to families and regular shoppers.

Hussain Shopping Mall

A highly-rated option with a 4.7 rating, Hussain Shopping Mall is famous for branded apparel, footwear, electronics, and modern shopping experience with a focus on quality and variety.

Sapna Shopping Mall

Located in the nearby town of Kagaznagar, this mall is a good option for a wider selection of stores, including clothing, electronics, accessories, and specialty items, attracting shoppers looking for variety beyond Adilabad.

Getting Around Within Adilabad

Adilabad offers several options to move around the town and nearby areas conveniently.

  • Bus Services: TSRTC and private buses connect key areas within Adilabad and nearby towns like Kagaznagar and Nirmal.
  • Auto-rickshaws: Widely available for short trips within the town.
  • Cabs & Taxi Services: Ola and local taxi operators provide point-to-point travel within Adilabad.
  • Two-wheelers & Bike Rentals: Available for local commuting and quick travel.
  • Railway: Adilabad Railway Station connects to major cities like Hyderabad, Nizamabad, and Warangal.

Best Time to Visit Adilabad

Novemberto February

These months offer pleasant and cool weather in Adilabad, ideal for sightseeing, exploring local markets, visiting waterfalls, and enjoying outdoor activities comfortably.
