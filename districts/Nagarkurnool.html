NAGARKURNOOL ‚Äî Where Heritage Meets Nature

Nagarkurnool is a district in southern Telangana known for its rich history, natural beauty, and cultural diversity. Formed in 2016 during the reorganization of Telangana districts, it has quickly grown into an important administrative and agricultural region. The district is surrounded by forests, hills, and scenic landscapes, with the **Nallamala Forest** and **Srisailam Tiger Reserve** lying close by, making it an attractive area for nature enthusiasts. Agriculture is the backbone of the region, with major crops like paddy, groundnut, cotton, and pulses supporting the local economy. Nagarkurnool town serves as the district headquarters and is a hub for education, trade, and government services. The area also carries historical importance, with several ancient temples, forts, and culturally significant sites found across its mandals. With improving infrastructure, connectivity, and public services, Nagarkurnool continues to develop while preserving its traditional rural charm.

Top Attractions in Nagarkurnool

Heritage monuments, lakes, museums, temples, zoos and cultural hubs ‚Äî Hyderabad has it all.

Mallela Theertham Waterfalls

Mallela Theertham, deep inside the Nallamala Forest, is a magnificent waterfall where the Krishna River drops from a 150-foot cliff into a forest pool. Reaching the site involves descending around 350 steps surrounded by greenery. The area is believed to have spiritual significance, as sages once performed penance here. During the monsoon, the fall roars to life, creating a misty, enchanting view. Monkeys, birds, and butterflies add to the natural charm. The serene environment makes it a favorite spot for nature lovers, photographers, and devotees alike.

Uma Maheshwaram Temple

Located near Mannanur in the Nallamala range, Uma Maheshwaram Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati. It is considered the northern gateway to the Srisailam Temple. Built on a hillside, the temple is surrounded by dense forest, natural springs, and caves. The nearby ‚ÄúPapanasanam‚Äù stream is believed to cleanse sins. The serene setting and gentle forest breeze create a meditative atmosphere. Ideal for both pilgrims and trekkers, the temple combines spiritual grace with the natural grandeur of Telangana‚Äôs Nallamala hills.

Somasila Temple Complex &Reservoir

Somasila, located along the Krishna River, is famous for its cluster of ancient Shiva temples and the scenic Somasila Reservoir. The site combines faith, heritage, and natural charm. During the monsoon, the riverbanks become lush and vibrant, and boating facilities attract visitors. Festivals like Maha Shivaratri and Karthika Deepam are celebrated with grandeur. The calm river, temple bells, and chirping birds make it a serene retreat for devotees and tourists alike. Somasila beautifully represents Telangana‚Äôs harmony between spirituality and nature.

Kollapur Palace and Temples

Kollapur, once a princely state under the Nizams, features a historic palace, temples, and old town streets reflecting Telangana‚Äôs royal past. The Kollapur Palace showcases Indo-European architecture with arched gateways and courtyards. Nearby temples like Sangameshwara and Venugopala Swamy add spiritual depth. The Krishna River nearby enhances the region‚Äôs charm. Visitors can explore the palace, interact with local artisans, and enjoy the blend of heritage, culture, and countryside life that defines the district‚Äôs character.

Singotam Fort and Temples

The historical area of Singotam is located in the Kollapur Mandal of the Nagarkurnool district. While remnants of a minor Singotam Fort exist, the location is most famous for the ancient Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple. Built by the Jetprole Rajas (Surabhi dynasty), the site takes its name from the founder, King Singamanaidu. The temple is picturesquely situated on the banks of the large Singasamudram reservoir, which forms the centerpiece of the region. This spot, combining historical ruins, spiritual significance, and natural beauty, remains a key pilgrimage site, especially during its annual festival.

Nagarkurnool Mini Tank Bund

The Nagarkurnool Mini Tank Bund is a premier urban rejuvenation project, transforming the historical Kesari Samudram irrigation tank into a vibrant recreational space. Sanctioned with a substantial budget, its primary goal is to provide town dwellers with a beautiful, relaxing environment. Key features include a newly developed Necklace Road encircling the water body, improved lighting for evening strolls, and dedicated facilities like a cycling track and recreation center to promote fitness. The presence of a Buddha statue adds to the aesthetic appeal, making it a popular hub for morning walks, family outings, and a relaxing break from city life. It stands as a jewel of the town, enhancing the quality of urban life.

Octopus View Point

The Octopus View Point is a spectacular natural lookout within the Amrabad Tiger Reserve in the Nagarkurnool district of Telangana. Named for the way the Krishna River backwaters appear from above, spreading out like the tentacles of an octopus among the Nallamala hills, it offers a breathtaking panoramic view. Located on the Hyderabad-Srisailam road, this viewpoint is a major highlight for eco-tourists. It provides a stunning perspective of the dense forest canopy, deep river gorges, and the massive reservoir, making it an essential stop for nature photography and experiencing the raw beauty of the protected wilderness area.

Nallamala Forest

The Nallamala Forest is one of the largest stretches of undisturbed dry deciduous forest in South India, spanning districts in both Telangana (including Nagarkurnool) and Andhra Pradesh. It is home to the Nagarjunasagar-Srisailam Tiger Reserve, the country's largest, protecting a healthy population of Bengal tigers, leopards, and other diverse wildlife. The forest is characterized by rugged hills, dense jungle, and important water bodies like the Krishna River. It also holds immense spiritual significance, hosting the sacred pilgrimage town of Srisailam with its famous Jyotirlinga temple. It's a key destination for wildlife safaris, trekking, and nature exploration.

Madhava Swamy Temple Kollapur

The Madhava Swamy Temple in Kollapur, Nagarkurnool district, is a stunning example of heritage conservation. Originally built in the 16th century A.D. by King Sri Surabhi Madhava Rayalu of the Jetprole Rajas, the temple stood on the banks of the Krishna River at Manchalakatta. Due to the construction of the Srisailam Dam, the entire structure was painstakingly dismantled, moved, and successfully transplanted at its current location in 1989. The temple‚Äôs beautiful Dravidian architecture is preserved, featuring elaborate carvings of the Dasa-Avatars of Lord Vishnu, making it a powerful testament to both history and engineering.

Alampur Jogulamba Temple

Situated at the confluence of the Tungabhadra and Krishna rivers, Alampur is home to the Jogulamba Temple ‚Äî one of the 18 Shakti Peethas ‚Äî and the ancient Navabrahma Temples built by the Chalukyas. The architecture features ornate sandstone carvings and detailed Dravidian towers. Devotees come for both spiritual and historical reasons. The riverside view, temple ponds, and peaceful ambiance make it one of the most revered and visually stunning destinations in southern Telangana.

Culture & Festivals

The culture of Nagarkurnool is a blend of ancient traditions, influences from various ruling dynasties, and the rich natural environment of the Nallamala Forest. Its traditions are rooted in the larger Telangana cultural context, with a distinct regional flavor due to its historical legacy and tribal communities

  • Languages: Telugu, Urdu, Hindi, lambadi and English are widely spoken.
  • Heritage: Nagarkurnool's heritage is ancient temples, forts, Nallamala forests, and diverse dynasties.
  • Major Festivals: Ramzan, Bathukamma, Bonalu, Dussehra, Diwali, Sankranti, Ugadi, Eid..

Getting Around Within Nagarkurnool

Nagarkurnool has multiple options for moving around the city comfortably and affordably.

  • City Buses: TSRTC operates extensive city buses including Pushpak airport services.
  • Cabs & Bike Taxis: Ola, Uber and Rapido work across most parts of the city.
  • Auto-rickshaws: Widely available and convenient for short-distance travel.
