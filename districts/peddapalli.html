Dhulikatta Buddhist Stupa

The Dhulikatta Buddhist Stupa, located in Dhulikatta village, Peddapalli district, Telangana, dates back to around the second century BCE. It features a circular brick drum, a dome, and ayaka platforms on four sides, with a lower circumambulatory path. Carved limestone slabs, including the Muchalinda Naga protecting the Buddha, highlight its historical and artistic significance. As of 2025, the site is recognized as a key heritage monument and has gained wider attention through commemorative events. Visitors can reach Dhulikatta by road, with the nearest railway station at Peddapalli. Getting Around Within Peddapalli

Peddapalli offers simple and convenient transport options for moving around the town and nearby areas.

  • City Buses: TSRTC runs regular buses connecting Peddapalli with nearby towns like Ramagundam, Manthani and Karimnagar.
  • Cabs & Bike Taxis: Services like Rapido and local cab operators are available in and around the town.
  • Auto-rickshaws: Widely available for short trips within the town and to nearby markets.
  • Local Transport: Shared autos and local jeeps are commonly used for village routes and daily commuting.
